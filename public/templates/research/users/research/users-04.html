Millennials are already gathering data from multiple sources and managing complexity in a far more efficient way than previous generations. They are looking for new tools that are less directional and allow for great peer-sharing and collaboration.

  • Attention has been given to the need to develop for mobile first but consideration should be given to what happens after the paradigm is established and mobile becomes ubiquitous.
    • Data optimization should happen regardless of the device - soon, tablets, phablets, smart TVs and even home appliances will blur the line between device mobility and user-intent. A phone won’t mean multi-tasking, on-the-go and distracted. A tablet won’t mean, companion device, surfing to supplement desktop tasks.
  • Data will need to be optimized not to device, but to user-chosen intent. Every tier, from simple to complex will need to be though-out on all devices to support this integration and collaboration, which will be leverage through NFC technology.Â
