The era in which business owners had to be persuaded to create a website is long gone. Now, companies of all sizes are souping up their websites with online communities, employing forums, blogs, and wikis. Whether a company sells pool supplies, electronics, or exercise equipment, an online community allows customers to share opinions and chat about common interests. This active customer participation establishes brand and company loyalty, says Matthew Lees, who specializes in customer communities and social networks as a vice president at the Patricia Seybold Group, a Boston-based consultancy.

Once customers get talking:

  • Companies can use the conversation to identify trends
  • Customers can help handle customer service inquiries
  • Companies can keep customers engaged in creative ways
  • Companies can keep a close eye on complaints and criticisms
