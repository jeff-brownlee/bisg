Interaction will be driven by user preferences. Users will be able to interact with data at any time, any place and any surface. How do we get there?

  • Explore direct interaction with data visualizations: All too often, data visualizations are relegated to a ‘report’ stage of the analytical process. There no longer is a need for reports to be static, just as there is no longer a need to accept the divide between the data and the visual representation.
  • Learn the lessons of gaming, data journalism and finance: Empower your users to create, customize and personalize their own experience through modules, add-ons, macros, scripts and APIs.
  • Prepare your User Experience for multiple interaction tools: Tour User Experience needs to consider that people interacting with the Portal may not be clicking and typing, but swiping, gesturing and voice-dictating. Make sure your Information Architects and User Experience Designers are anticipating this in designs.
